ℹ️ How to read this chart

What it shows: Each cell represents a drug (column) × symptom (row) pair. Color indicates how much better (green) or worse (red) that drug performs compared to the baseline rate for that symptom.

Metric definitions

Co-occurrence Significant Improvement Lift (default)
Among all reports where the patient was taking this drug and had this symptom, what % achieved "significant improvement" or "complete resolution" — minus the baseline rate for all patients with that symptom. Positive = better than average.
Co-occurrence Any Improvement Lift
Same as above but counts any improvement (mild, moderate, significant, or resolved) vs. baseline. Broader signal, less specific.
Co-occurrence Significant Improvement Rate
Raw % of co-occurring reports that achieved significant improvement or resolution (not relative to baseline). Useful for absolute comparison.
Attributed Significant Improvement Lift
Only counts outcomes where the patient explicitly credited this drug for the symptom change. Smaller N but stronger causal signal. Lift is vs. the attributed baseline across all drugs for that symptom.

Controls

Min co-occur N
Minimum number of reports where the patient was taking the drug and had the symptom. Higher = fewer drugs shown but more reliable estimates.
Sort drugs by
Hover each button for details. Sorts drug columns by the selected metric using different aggregation strategies.

Tooltip

Hover any cell to see: co-occurrence N, significant improvement rate and lift, any improvement rate and lift, attributed stats (if available), and symptom baseline rates.

Side panel (click)

Click a drug name (column header) to see that drug's performance across all symptoms, ranked by the current metric, with total N and average.

Click a symptom name (row label) to see all drugs ranked for that symptom, plus the symptom's baseline rates.

Click a cell to see full detail: rankings (where this drug stands among all drugs for the symptom, and vice versa), all co-occurrence and attributed stats, symptom baseline, and a mini leaderboard of top drugs for that symptom.

Color scale

Lift metrics (all three): diverging red → yellow → green. The scale range is fixed across all lift metrics and N thresholds — the same color always means the same magnitude of lift, making views directly comparable.

Rate metric: sequential yellow → green (0% to the global maximum rate). Also fixed regardless of N threshold.

Dark cells = no data for that pair at the current N threshold.
