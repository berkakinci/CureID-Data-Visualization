ℹ️ How to read this chart

What it shows: Each bar represents one drug. Each segment represents one patient report, quantized into an outcome bucket based on the report's average attributed symptom score. This gives each patient one vote regardless of how many symptoms they reported.

Scoring: Each symptom outcome is scored (resolution=+3, significant=+2, moderate=+1, mild=+0.5, unchanged=0, worsening=-0.5 to -2). A report's score is the average across all symptoms the patient attributed to that drug. The average is then quantized back to the nearest outcome label for the bar segment color.

Quantization thresholds: avg ≥ 2.5 → Resolved, ≥ 1.5 → Significant, ≥ 0.75 → Moderate, ≥ 0.25 → Mild, ≥ -0.25 → Unchanged, < -0.25 → Worsened.

Controls

Sort by
Orders the drug list by the selected metric. "Sum of scores" rewards both quality and volume (default). "Average score" gives each patient equal weight. Percentage sorts rank by the fraction of reports in that outcome tier or better.
Min reports
Minimum number of unique patient reports for a drug to appear. Higher = fewer drugs shown but more reliable estimates.
Normalize bars
When checked, all bars fill the full width (proportional view). When unchecked, bar length scales with number of reports — longer bars = more data. Auto-toggles based on sort selection.

Drill-down

Click any bar to open a panel listing individual case reports for that drug, with demographics, illness duration, and per-symptom outcomes attributed to the drug. Cases are sorted by their average score.
